# Forge Fire

The name *FunscriptForge* draws on the language of the blacksmith's forge — a place where raw material is shaped into something precise and purposeful through controlled heat and skilled technique.

The term appears widely in smithing contexts and even in fantasy adaptations, where "Forge Fire" can refer to the heat associated with a forge or tools imbued with that heat.

## The anatomy of a forge fire

- **The heart** — the hottest central zone where metal is placed to reach forging temperature.
- **The sweet spot** — the ideal region of the fire for even, controlled heating.
- **The firepot** — the container or depression that holds the burning fuel; sometimes people casually refer to the fire itself as "the fire in the firepot."
- **The coke/coal mound** — in solid-fuel forges, smiths build a mound of coke or coal around the heart to shape airflow and temperature.

## The metaphor in FunscriptForge

A raw funscript is like unworked metal — it has potential but needs shaping. The forge fire gives it form:

- The **heart** is the peak intensity phrase — where the script reaches its highest energy.
- The **sweet spot** is the phrase region with ideal rhythm and amplitude balance — what makes a great estim session feel right.
- The **firepot** is the underlying beat structure — the container that holds the whole script's energy.
- The **coke/coal mound** is the BPM buildup — how tempo accumulates and shapes what comes next.
